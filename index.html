Making the change: Childcare to TechnologyChallenge vs. Knowledge

During my childhood, I grew up playing with dolls, looking after & pushing them around in a pram, dreaming of the day I would be old enough to become a nurse or be looking after young children, helping them to grow and enjoy their early years, whilst developing their young minds. When I left school at 18, with good GCSEs and A-levels and by the time I'd left, I was already working, with a regular supermarket job in the day, and a bar job in the evening, whilst I studied to become qualified in working with children.

At age 20, I had to put my studies on hold, as I became a mum myself, to a wonderful daughter called Poppy. (She is 10 now - how time flies!) and by the time i had settled into motherhood, battling the school run every morning, i had my second child, a son called Isaac (or Whirlwind as we like to call him!)

After going through motherhood again, from the very beginning, I resumed my Childhood studies, luckily i had completed my level 2 certificate before i’d had my daughter, so i could go on to level 3 with confidence, Trust me when i say juggling an education and children is definitely not easy! - Not by a long shot! But after 18 months I had finally completed my studies. Fast forward 5 years, i was 28 - in the prime of my career, i was a Room leader for ages 3-5 year olds, had two wonderful children, so why did i feel complacent? Like I was missing something?

That’s because i was, i was missing the challenge, the get up in the morning, and the chance to add my own ideas, and artistic flare to something i had created, and was able to proudly showcase to lots of people, who were also able to contribute their own idea/take on projects, allowing me to see things from a different perspective. The difference been, that in Childcare you need the knowledge to be able to follow the educational framework & teach children, as rewarding as this was. I found myself craving challenges on a daily basis.

As i researched Technology and Web Development regularly in the evenings initially, from the second i finally managed to sit down in the evening, i found myself becoming drawn more and more to it, as i signed up to web development sites, and began taking part in challenges and tutorials set by tutors and experts, I knew this is the sector where i could discover the challenges i needed to engage my brain, getting me thinking about the 3 W’s: What I would be learning next, Where could i use my newly learned skills, and When was the right time to put them into practice. In Autumn, I am beginning a Computer Science degree.

I ended up leaving the job i thought was my dream one, to take myself down another career path, where my mind and decision making were being challenged almost daily, i made new friends on LinkedIn and Twitter, who were always on hand to help out with any questions when i had days where i felt like giving up, you may be familiar with those, you know, when that code you thought was right, returns a “Syntax Error”, or your console doesn't display the message you thought it would, and you spend all day looking at it, to discover 50 lines up you forgot to close a >div< tag. Yes, those kinds of days.

I soon discovered that everyone, however experienced they were, are all on the same journey. We are all here to help and support each other along the way.

If anyone says learning code and various platforms to execute it is easy, they are lying to you.. But is Learning Web development, and becoming an engineer achievable? Absolutely!
